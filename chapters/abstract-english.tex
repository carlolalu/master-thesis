The aim of this master's thesis is to derive the effective equations for a microscopic system (a system of stochastic differential equations) for \emph{chemotaxis} of $N$ amoebas attracted to the molecule of cAMP (cyclic adenosine monophosphate) that they released in random pulses.
The attraction is given by a Gaussian-like potential, and the creation of cAMP particles follows a Poisson process.
This microscopic model is inspired from \cite{ana}, which is also the main source of the techniques that we used in the proof, together with \cite{peter-boers}.
Furthermore, we discuss the similarities and differences of the macroscopic description found with the classic model for chemotaxis, namely the \emph{Keller-Segel} equation.
