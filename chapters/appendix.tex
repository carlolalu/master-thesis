\section{Is the casual model just a relabeling?}

These are the questions:

\begin{itemize}
\item Given the deterministic parenting model (\ie the processes $(\hmactive_t^p)_p$ indexing the cAMP particles), can I derive the casual parenting model, \ie write the system \ref{ch2:model:casual-parenting} with a process $\hmactive_t$ and a parenting function $\Parentof$ with the required distributions and characteristics? 
\item Given the the casual parenting model, \ie the process $\hmactive_t$ and the parenting function $\Parentof$, can I derive the deterministic parenting model, \ie write the system \ref{ch2:model:deterministic-parenting} with processes $(\hmactive_t^p)_p$?
\end{itemize}

If the answer to both is yes, then the models are equivalent.

\subsection{From deterministic to casual parenting}

The process $\hmactive_t \sim \Gma_{m, N\lambda}$ (and therefore its birthtimes $\btime_m$) was already derived in the definition of \ref{ch2:model:casual-parenting}.
In such case the birth time $\btime_m$ is the spawning time of the $m$-th molecule spawned in the deterministic system.
Which amoeba is spawning such particle?

\begin{equation}
\Parentof'(m) := p \quad \text{s.t. }\amba^p \text{ is the parent of } \camp^m
\end{equation}

The question is, if such function respects the two assumptions we made on $\Parentof$ for \ref{ch2:model:casual-parenting}:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\invquest \quad \Parentof'(m) \sim \Unf_{\{1,\ldots,N\}} \quad ?$ 
\item $\invquest \quad \forall m_1 \neq m_2.\Parentof'(m_1) \indep \Parentof'(m_2) \quad ?$
\end{itemize}

Let us answer to the first one:

\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\Prb (\Parentof'(m) = p) 
&= \Prb (\exists g \in \N. \btime_m = \btime^{p,g} )
\\
&= \sum_{g=1}^{\infty} \Prb (\btime_m = \btime^{p,g})
\\
&= \sum_{g=1}^{\infty} \Prb (\hmactive_{\btime^{p,g}} = m)
\\
&= \sum_{g=1}^{\infty} \Prb (\hmactive_{\btime^{p,g}}^p = g, \sum_{k\neq p} \hmactive_{\btime^{p,g}}^k = m - g)
\\
&= \sum_{g=1}^{\infty} \Prb (g = g, \sum_{k\neq p} \hmactive_{\btime^{p,g}}^k = m - g)
\\
&= \sum_{g=1}^{\infty} \int \Prb( \sum_{k\neq p} \hmactive_{\btime^{p,g}}^k = m - g) 
	\big| \btime^{p,g} = s) d\Prb(\btime^{p,g} = s)
\\
&= \sum_{g=1}^{\infty} \int \Pois_{(N-1)s\lambda}(\{ m-g\})\1_{m-g \geq 0} 
	\underbrace{d\Prb(\btime^{p,g} = s)}_{:=\Zhi(p,g,s) ds}
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

And since $\Zhi(p,g,s)=d\Gma_{g,\lambda}$, it is independent from $p$, we have that $\Prb (\Parentof'(m) = p) = const(m)$ and thus uniformly distirbuted on $\{1,\ldots,N\}$.

The second condition is

\begin{equation*}
\invquest
	\quad \forall p,q . \forall m,n\in\N .
		\quad \Prb (\Parentof'(m) = p,\Parentof'(n) = q) =^{?} \Prb (\Parentof'(m) = p) \Prb(\Parentof'(n) = q) = \frac{1}{N^2}
?
\end{equation*}\label{app:casual-determ:condition-indep-parentof}

\section{What is a solution to the deterministic parenting model, formally}\label{app:formal-def-solution}

First we define the space of configurations of cAMP molecules spawned by a single amoeba.
Here the $\star$ means that a particle has not been spawned yet.
We must take only sequences where only the first coordinates (finitely) are different from $\star$.

\begin{definition}
Define the space of the cAMP particles spawned by an amoeba as:
\begin{equation}
\R^d_{camp}:=\boxplus_{g=1}^\N (\{\star\} \sqcup \R^d) := \{x = (x_g)_{g\in\N} : \exists G\in\N_0.\forall g\geq G. x_g =\star\}
\end{equation}
Define the space of configurations for all particles as
\begin{equation}
Conf:=(\R^d)^N \times\big(\R^d_{camp}\big)^N
\end{equation}
For each configuration of particles $x\in Conf$ sign its coordinates as 
\begin{equation*}
\begin{aligned}
x &= (x^1, \ldots, x^N, 
	&(x^{1,g})_{g\in\N}, \ldots, 
	&(x^{p,g})_{g\in\N}, \ldots, 
	&(x^{N,g})_{g\in\N})
\\
&= (a^1, \ldots, a^N, 
	&c^{1,1}, c^{1,2}, \ldots, 
	&c^{p,1},\ldots, 
	&c^{N,1}, \ldots)
\end{aligned}
\end{equation*}
where the absence of a coordinate in the list means that such coordinate is set to $\star$.
\end{definition}

This definition is important to have processes and function with domains and codomains which are constant throughout time.
For example:

\begin{definition} Let $S$ be the space of strictly increasing sequences on $\R+$. Then define $\drift: \R_+ \times Conf \times S^N \longrightarrow Conf$ as
\begin{equation}
\left\{
\begin{aligned}
&\drift_p(t,x,(\hmactive_t^p)_p) = \frac{1}{N} \sum _{p = 1}^{N} \sum_{g = 1}^{\hmactive_s^p} \attraction(x^p - x^{p,g})
	&& 1\leq p \leq N
\\
&\drift_{p,g}(t,x) = 0
	&& \text{if } \btime^{p,g} \leq t
\\
&\drift_{p,g}(t,x) = \star
	&& \text{otherwise}
\end{aligned}
\right.
\end{equation}
Let $\diffusion:Conf\longrightarrow Conf$ be defined as
\begin{equation}
\left\{
\begin{aligned}
&\diffusion_p:= \sigma_A
&
\\
&\diffusion_{p,g} = \sigma_C
	&& \text{if } \btime^{p,g} \leq t
\\
&\diffusion_{p,g} = \star
	&& \text{otherwise}
\end{aligned}
\right.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}\label{ch3:formal-def-drift-diffus}

Now we need to see what does it mean to formally solve a piecewise define SDE with a quasi-brownian motion as ours:

\begin{definition}
Given a Brownian motion $B$ and a stopping time $T$ we define the quasi Brownian motion
\begin{equation}
\quasibm_t^{B,T} :=
\left\{
\begin{aligned}
& 0 
& t<T
\\
&B_{t} - B_{T}
& t \geq T
\end{aligned}
\right.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

Notice then that in the system \ref{ch2:model:deterministic-parenting} we have that
\begin{equation}
\quasibm^{B^{p,g}, T^{p,g}} = \quasibm^{p,g}
\end{equation}
