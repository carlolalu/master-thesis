Till now we treated the simplest model, where at the moment of spawning the cAMP was a lonely molecule simply roaming around of \Bm.
This is though very far from reality, where the cAMP is released in waves expanding radially around the amoeba.

Consider the same Poisson process $(\hmactive_t^p)_p$ and their correspondent $\hmactive_t$, with the respective birth times.
Let $\NumCampRelease \in \N$ be the (deterministic) number of cAMP molecules being spawned at each birth time $\btime^{p,g}=\btime_m$ and let $\frac{\cdrift}{N} \in \R_+$ be the (constant deterministic) scale-invariant modulus of their drift velocity at each of these instants.
Then, at each $\btime^{p,g}=\btime_m$ we would release the particles

\begin{equation*}
	\{ \camp^{p,g,1}, \ldots, \camp^{p,g,l}, \ldots, \camp^{p,g,\NumCampRelease}\} = \{ \camp^{m,1}, \ldots, \camp^{m,l},\ldots, \camp^{m,\NumCampRelease}\} 
\end{equation*}

While the dynamic equation for the $N$ amoebas remains the same, at this point the dynamic for the cAMP changes.
First, each of them will have its quasi-\Bm.
Besides that, each $\camp^{p,g,l}=\camp^{m,l}$ should drift in a constant random direction $\cgonia_{p,g,l}=\cgonia^{m,l} \sim \Unf(\Sphere^d)$, \ie uniformly-distributed on the $d$-dimensional sphere.

Then the model is described as

\begin{equation}
	\left\{
	\begin{aligned}
		(\hmactive^p_t)_{p=1}^N 
		&\text{ \iid and } \sim \Pois_{\lambda t}
		\qquad\indep\qquad
		(\cgonia_{p,g,l})_{p,g,l=1}^{N,+\infty, \NumCampRelease}\  \text{ \iid and } \sim \Unf(\Sphere^d)
		&
		\\
		 \btime^{p,g} &:= \inf \{ t : \hmactive^p_t \geq g \}
		&
		\\
		\amba_t^p 
		&= \amba_0^p 
		+ \int_0^t \frac{1}{N} \sum _{l = 1}^{\NumCampRelease} \sum _{q = 1}^{N} \sum_{g = 1}^{\hmactive_s^q} \attraction(\amba_s^p - \camp_s^{q,g,l}) d s
		+ \sigma^A B_t^p
		&
		\\
		\camp_t^{p,g,l} 
		&\qquad\text{does not exist} 
		&\text{if} \quad t < \btime^{p,g}
		\\
		\camp_t^{p,g,l}
		&= \amba_{\btime^{p,g}}^p + \int_{0}^t \1_{\geq \btime^{p,g}}(s)  \frac{\cdrift}{N}  \cgonia_{p,g,l} d s
		+\sigma_C \quasibm_t^{p,g,l}
		&\text{if} \quad t \geq \btime^{p,g}
		\\
		&= \amba_{\btime^{p,g}}^p + (\btime^{p,g} - t)\frac{\cdrift}{N}  \cgonia_{p,g,l} d s 
		+\sigma_C \quasibm_t^{p,g,l}
			&
	\end{aligned}
	\right.
\end{equation}\label{ch7:model:pulsing-waves}

or, using the process $\hmactive_t$:

\begin{equation}
	\left\{
	\begin{aligned}
		\hmactive_t
		&\sim \Pois_{\lambda t}
		\qquad\indep\qquad
		(\cgonia_{m,l})_{m,l=1}^{+\infty, \NumCampRelease}\  \text{ \iid and } \sim \Unf(\Sphere^d)
		&
		\\
		\btime_m &:= \inf \{ t : \hmactive_t \geq m \}
		&
		\\
		\amba_t^p 
		&= \amba_0^p 
		+ \int_0^t \frac{1}{N} \sum _{l = 1}^{\NumCampRelease} \sum_{m = 1}^{\hmactive_s} \attraction(\amba_s^p - \camp_s^{m,l}) d s
		+ \sigma^A B_t^p
		&
		\\
		\camp_t^{m,l} 
		&\qquad\text{does not exist} 
		&\text{if} \quad t < \btime_m
		\\
		\camp_t^{m,l}
		&= \amba_{\btime_{m}}^p + \int_{0}^t \1_{\geq \btime_m}(s)  \frac{\cdrift}{N}  \cgonia_{m,l} d s
		+\sigma_C \quasibm_t^{m,l}
		&\text{if} \quad t \geq \btime^{m}
		\\
		&= \amba_{\btime_{m}}^p + (\btime_m - t)\frac{\cdrift}{N}  \cgonia_{m,l} d s 
		+\sigma_C \quasibm_t^{m,l}
		&
	\end{aligned}
	\right.
\end{equation}\label{ch7:model:pulsing-waves:alternative}

We could eventually ask fro more regularity to obtain deterministically that the mesh of cAMP leaving the amoeba is equidistributed, \ie we would have that only $\cgonia_{p,g,1}$ is random, and all the other unit vectors $\cgonia_{p,g,l}$ would be simply arranged on $\Sphere^d$ in such way as to be equidistant from each others, thus by rotating $\cgonia_{p,g,1}$ of a fixed angle along a fixed axis.
In two dimensions this would mean

\begin{equation*}
	\cgonia_{p,g,l} := \text{deterministic rotation depending on $l$} (\cgonia_{p,g,l})
\end{equation*}
